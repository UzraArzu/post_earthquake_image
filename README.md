# post_earthquake_image
Post earthquake Image Dataset

Our dataset consists of UAV-based post-earthquake images gathered from various online platforms, including Twitter, YouTube, and Google Images. Unlike many prior studies limited to a single region, this dataset covers multiple countries and cities, increasing its diversity and generalizability. It includes images from the 2023 Kahramanmaraş and Hatay earthquakes, the 2020 İzmir and Elazığ earthquakes in Turkey, the 2015 Kathmandu earthquake in Nepal, the 2022 Afghanistan earthquake, the 2023 Morocco earthquake, the 2016 Taiwan earthquake, as well as earthquakes in Japan (1995) and Italy (2016).

In addition to post-earthquake UAV imagery, the dataset also contains normal images from the same regions. Beyond conventional high-altitude, top-down UAV views, it incorporates oblique aerial images captured at different altitudes and angles. The dataset represents varying levels of structural damage, from minor impacts to total collapse and debris-filled areas, providing a comprehensive view of post-earthquake conditions.

The dataset contains 4,004 images in total, comprising 2,751 post-earthquake images and 1,253 normal images. For the classification task, all images were resized to 224 × 224 pixels and converted to RGB format. Manual annotation was performed to ensure precise and reliable labeling.
